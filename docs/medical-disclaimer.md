# Pioneer Nurse -- Medical and Educational Disclaimer

**Effective Date:** March 15, 2026

---

## Educational Study Tool

Pioneer Nurse is designed as a supplementary study tool for nursing students enrolled in Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) programs. The app provides educational content including AI-powered tutoring, drug reference cards, practice questions, spaced repetition flashcards, and clinical preparation templates to support coursework and exam preparation.

## Not Medical Advice

**Pioneer Nurse is an educational study tool. It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult qualified healthcare professionals for medical decisions.**

Nothing in this application should be interpreted as:

- A medical diagnosis or clinical assessment
- A recommendation for treatment, medication, or therapeutic intervention
- A substitute for the professional judgment of licensed healthcare providers
- Guidance for the care of actual patients in any clinical setting

## Not a Substitute for Clinical Instruction

Pioneer Nurse is not a substitute for:

- **Clinical instruction** from qualified nursing faculty and preceptors
- **Course textbooks** and official curriculum materials assigned by your program
- **Institutional protocols** and clinical site-specific guidelines and procedures
- **Professional medical advice** from licensed physicians, nurse practitioners, pharmacists, and other healthcare providers
- **Evidence-based nursing practice** standards published by professional nursing organizations

Students should always defer to their instructors, clinical preceptors, and institutional policies when there is any discrepancy between app content and official course materials or clinical site expectations.

## AI-Generated Content

The AI Tutor feature uses artificial intelligence (Anthropic's Claude) to generate study explanations and answer nursing-related questions. AI-generated content has inherent limitations:

- **AI can produce inaccurate information.** Responses may contain errors, outdated information, incomplete explanations, or misinterpretations of clinical concepts. AI models can generate plausible-sounding but incorrect statements.
- **AI does not have clinical judgment.** The AI tutor cannot assess patient conditions, interpret diagnostic findings in context, or account for the complexity of real clinical situations.
- **AI responses are not peer-reviewed.** Unlike textbooks and clinical guidelines, AI-generated content has not undergone formal review by nursing faculty or clinical experts.
- **AI knowledge has limitations.** The AI model's training data has a cutoff date and may not reflect the most current evidence, guidelines, or pharmacological information.

All AI-generated content should be verified against authoritative nursing resources, including:

- Current edition course textbooks
- Peer-reviewed nursing journals
- Evidence-based clinical practice guidelines
- Faculty lectures and course materials
- Institutional drug formularies and protocols

## Drug and Pharmacology Information

Drug information provided in Pioneer Nurse -- including drug cards, hold parameters, dosages, mechanisms of action, nursing considerations, and side effects -- is intended for educational reference only.

**Critical considerations:**

- Drug dosages, hold parameters, and administration protocols **vary by institution, patient population, age, weight, renal function, hepatic function, and clinical context.** The information in this app reflects general nursing education content and may not match your specific clinical site's protocols.
- Hold parameters presented in drug cards are general guidelines commonly taught in nursing education. **Always verify hold parameters with your clinical site's specific protocols** before withholding any medication in a clinical setting.
- Drug information can become outdated as new research emerges, FDA guidelines change, and institutional formularies are updated. **Always verify against current pharmacological references** such as a current drug handbook, your institution's formulary, or resources like DailyMed, Lexicomp, or Micromedex.
- Never administer, withhold, or modify any medication based solely on information from this app. **Always follow your institution's medication administration policies** and consult with the prescribing provider or pharmacist when you have questions.

## Practice Questions and Study Content

NCLEX-style practice questions, rationales, and study content are designed to help students prepare for examinations. This content:

- Is intended for self-assessment and knowledge reinforcement only
- Does not guarantee any specific exam outcome or passing score
- Should be used alongside -- not instead of -- comprehensive NCLEX review courses, textbooks, and faculty-guided preparation
- May not reflect the exact format, difficulty, or content distribution of the actual NCLEX-RN examination
